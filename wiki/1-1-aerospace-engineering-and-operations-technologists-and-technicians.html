Aerospace Engineering and Operations Technologists and Technicians

Job Title: Aerospace Engineering and Operations Technologists and Technicians
(航空航天工程与运行技术员)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $73,580 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~11,000
10-Year Job Growth: 6% (2022-2032), ~700 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Aerospace Engineering and Operations Technologists and Technicians assist aerospace engineers in designing, testing, and producing aircraft, spacecraft, and related systems and equipment.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Operate and maintain test equipment, collect and analyze data, assist in the design and development of aerospace systems
  • Collaborative: Work with engineers and other technicians, participate in team meetings, document results
  • Supportive: Prepare reports, maintain records, ensure compliance with safety standards

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Wind tunnels, flight simulators, computer-aided design (CAD) systems
  • Software: MATLAB, LabVIEW, AutoCAD, SolidWorks
  • Programming: Python, C++ (for simulation and automation)

Common Specializations

  • Flight Test Technician
  • Avionics Technician
  • Propulsion Systems Technician

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in engineering technology or related field
  • Certifications: Optional, but certifications from the National Institute for Certification in Engineering Technologies (NICET) or similar are valued
  • Experience: Internships or cooperative education programs are highly recommended

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Technical drawing, electronics, data analysis, troubleshooting
  • Soft Skills: Attention to detail, teamwork, communication, problem-solving
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, reliability, adaptability

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Technician → Senior Technician → Lead Technologist
  • Horizontal: Transition to aerospace engineering, quality assurance, or project management
  • Management vs. Expert: Team lead roles or deep technical specialization

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: NICET, FAA certifications
  • Books: "Introduction to Aerospace Engineering" by Stephen Corda
  • Competitions: NASA Student Launch, AIAA Design Competitions
  • Communities: AIAA, SAE International

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Troubleshooting scenarios, safety protocol questions, teamwork examples
  • Portfolio: Project reports, CAD drawings, test data analyses

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $60,000
  • Median: $73,580
  • P75: $90,000 (varies by region and industry)

Job Outlook

  • Steady demand in defense, commercial aviation, and space sectors
  • Automation risk is moderate due to hands-on and technical nature

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Seattle, Los Angeles, Houston
  • Major industries: Aerospace manufacturing, government, R&D

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and manufacturing settings
  • May involve shift work or overtime during testing phases
  • Safety: Strict adherence to safety protocols required
Related Occupations: Aerospace Engineer, Avionics Technician, Mechanical Engineering Technician
High skill overlap with electrical and mechanical tech roles
