Drafters

Job Title: Drafters
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $60,000 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~198,000
10-Year Job Growth: -3% (2022-2032), ~3,000 fewer jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Drafters create technical drawings and plans used by engineers and architects to build structures and products.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Create detailed technical drawings, convert engineering designs into blueprints, ensure accuracy and compliance
  • Collaborative: Work with engineers and architects, review and revise drawings based on feedback
  • Supportive: Maintain drawing libraries, prepare documentation, update existing drawings

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Computer workstations, plotters, 3D printers
  • Software: AutoCAD, SolidWorks, Revit, MicroStation, SketchUp
  • Programming: Basic scripting for automation (AutoLISP, VBA)

Common Specializations

  • Architectural Drafter
  • Mechanical Drafter
  • Electrical Drafter
  • Civil Drafter
  • Electronics Drafter

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in drafting or related field
  • Certifications: AutoCAD certification, industry-specific certifications
  • Experience: Internships or entry-level positions are valuable

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: CAD software, technical drawing, blueprint reading, mathematics
  • Soft Skills: Attention to detail, problem-solving, communication
  • Behavioral Traits: Precision, patience, analytical thinking

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Drafter → Senior Drafter → Lead Drafter → CAD Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as engineering technician or design
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: AutoCAD certification, SolidWorks certification
  • Books: "Technical Drawing" by Frederick E. Giesecke
  • Competitions: CAD design competitions
  • Communities: AUGI, local drafting societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: CAD software proficiency, technical drawing skills, project examples
  • Portfolio: Technical drawings, CAD models, project documentation

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $35,000
  • Median: $60,000
  • P75: $85,000 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Declining employment due to automation and outsourcing
  • Automation risk is high; CAD software continues to advance

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Texas, Michigan
  • Major industries: Engineering services, manufacturing, construction

Work Environment

  • Primarily office-based with computer workstations
  • May require overtime to meet project deadlines
  • Emphasis on accuracy and attention to detail
Related Occupations: Engineering Technician, CAD Operator, Design Technician.
High skill overlap with engineering support roles
