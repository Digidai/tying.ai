Electrical and Electronic Engineering Technologists and Technicians

Job Title: Electrical and Electronic Engineering Technologists and Technicians
(电气和电子工程技术员和技术专家)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $67,000 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~115,000
10-Year Job Growth: 2% (2022-2032), ~2,000 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Electrical and Electronic Engineering Technologists and Technicians assist electrical and electronics engineers in designing, developing, testing, and manufacturing electrical equipment and systems.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Test electrical equipment, troubleshoot circuits, assist in design and development
  • Collaborative: Work with engineers and other technicians, participate in team projects
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, maintain equipment, ensure safety compliance

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Oscilloscopes, multimeters, signal generators, power supplies
  • Software: MATLAB, LabVIEW, SPICE, AutoCAD Electrical
  • Programming: Python, C/C++ (for testing and automation)

Common Specializations

  • Electronics Technician
  • Power Systems Technician
  • Control Systems Technician
  • Telecommunications Technician

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in electrical engineering technology or related field
  • Certifications: Optional certifications in specific technologies or safety
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or entry-level positions are valuable

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Circuit design, electronics, testing, troubleshooting
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, teamwork, communication, attention to detail
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, safety consciousness, reliability

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Technician → Senior Technician → Lead Technologist → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as automation, robotics, or telecommunications
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: Electronics certifications, safety certifications
  • Books: "Electronics for Technicians" by Earl D. Gates
  • Competitions: IEEE Student competitions
  • Communities: IEEE, local engineering societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Circuit troubleshooting, safety protocols, teamwork examples
  • Portfolio: Project reports, circuit designs, test results, safety documentation

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $45,000
  • Median: $67,000
  • P75: $95,000 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Moderate growth with increasing automation and electronics in manufacturing
  • Automation risk is moderate; hands-on skills remain valuable

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Texas, Michigan
  • Major industries: Manufacturing, engineering services, utilities

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and manufacturing settings
  • May require shift work in manufacturing environments
  • Emphasis on safety and electrical hazard awareness
Related Occupations: Electrical Engineer, Electronics Engineer, Electrician
High skill overlap with electrical engineering and electronics
