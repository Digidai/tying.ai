Electrical and Electronics Engineers

Job Title: Electrical and Electronics Engineers
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $104,610 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~327,900
10-Year Job Growth: 5% (2022-2032), ~17,800 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Electrical and Electronics Engineers design, develop, test, and supervise the manufacturing of electrical equipment and electronic devices.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design electrical systems, develop electronic devices, test and validate designs
  • Collaborative: Work with multidisciplinary teams, manage projects, coordinate with clients
  • Supportive: Prepare technical documentation, conduct research, ensure compliance

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Oscilloscopes, spectrum analyzers, power supplies, test equipment
  • Software: MATLAB, Simulink, SPICE, AutoCAD Electrical, Altium Designer
  • Programming: C/C++, Python, Verilog, VHDL

Common Specializations

  • Power Systems Engineer
  • Control Systems Engineer
  • Electronics Engineer
  • Telecommunications Engineer
  • Robotics Engineer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in electrical engineering or related field
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license for higher responsibility roles
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or entry-level positions are highly valued

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Circuit design, power systems, electronics, mathematics
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, teamwork, communication, project management
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, creativity, attention to detail

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Senior Engineer → Principal Engineer → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as computer engineering, systems engineering, or consulting
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, IEEE certifications
  • Books: "Electrical Engineering: Principles and Applications" by Allan R. Hambley
  • Competitions: IEEE Student competitions, robotics competitions
  • Communities: IEEE, local engineering societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Circuit design problems, power systems analysis, project management
  • Portfolio: Circuit designs, project reports, technical papers, code samples

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $70,000
  • Median: $104,610
  • P75: $168,200 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Steady growth driven by renewable energy, automation, and electronics demand
  • Automation risk is low due to complex design and innovation requirements

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Texas, New York
  • Major industries: Engineering services, manufacturing, utilities

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and field work
  • May require travel to project sites and manufacturing facilities
  • Emphasis on safety and regulatory compliance
Related Occupations: Computer Hardware Engineer, Mechanical Engineer, Systems Engineer
High skill overlap with computer engineering and systems engineering
