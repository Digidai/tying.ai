Electro-mechanical and Mechatronics Technologists and Technicians

Job Title: Electro-mechanical and Mechatronics Technologists and Technicians
(机电一体化技术员和技术专家)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $70,000 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~54,000
10-Year Job Growth: 3% (2022-2032), ~1,500 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Electro-mechanical and Mechatronics Technologists and Technicians operate, test, and maintain automated, servo-mechanical, or electromechanical equipment.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Operate automated equipment, test electromechanical systems, troubleshoot and repair equipment
  • Collaborative: Work with engineers and other technicians, participate in equipment installation and maintenance
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, maintain equipment documentation, ensure safety compliance

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: PLCs, servo systems, robotics, test equipment, diagnostic tools
  • Software: PLC programming software, HMI software, CAD/CAM systems
  • Programming: Ladder logic, structured text, Python, C/C++

Common Specializations

  • Automation Technician
  • Robotics Technician
  • PLC Programmer
  • Servo Systems Technician
  • Mechatronics Specialist

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in mechatronics, electro-mechanical technology, or related field
  • Certifications: PLC certifications, robotics certifications, safety certifications
  • Experience: Internships, apprenticeships, or entry-level positions are valuable

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Automation, electronics, mechanical systems, programming
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, teamwork, communication, attention to detail
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, safety consciousness, reliability

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Technician → Senior Technician → Lead Technologist → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as robotics engineering, automation engineering, or systems integration
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PLC certifications, robotics certifications, safety certifications
  • Books: "Mechatronics: An Introduction" by Robert H. Bishop
  • Competitions: Robotics competitions, automation competitions
  • Communities: IEEE, local automation societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: PLC programming, troubleshooting scenarios, safety protocols
  • Portfolio: Project reports, programming examples, equipment documentation, safety records

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $45,000
  • Median: $70,000
  • P75: $100,000 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Moderate growth with increasing automation in manufacturing
  • Automation risk is moderate; hands-on skills remain valuable

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Michigan, California, Texas
  • Major industries: Manufacturing, engineering services, automation

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and manufacturing settings
  • May require shift work in manufacturing environments
  • Emphasis on safety and equipment reliability
Related Occupations: Electrical Engineer, Mechanical Engineer, Robotics Engineer
High skill overlap with electrical and mechanical engineering
