Environmental Engineering Technologists and Technicians

Job Title: Environmental Engineering Technologists and Technicians
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $62,000 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~42,000
10-Year Job Growth: 6% (2022-2032), ~2,500 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Environmental Engineering Technologists and Technicians apply theory and principles of environmental engineering to modify, test, and operate equipment and devices used in the prevention, control, and remediation of environmental problems.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Monitor environmental conditions, operate pollution control equipment, collect and analyze samples
  • Collaborative: Work with environmental engineers, government agencies, and regulatory bodies
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, maintain equipment, ensure regulatory compliance

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Air quality monitors, water quality analyzers, soil testing equipment, sampling devices
  • Software: GIS software, data analysis tools, modeling software
  • Programming: Python, R (for data analysis and modeling)

Common Specializations

  • Air Quality Technician
  • Water Quality Technician
  • Waste Management Technician
  • Environmental Monitoring Specialist
  • Remediation Technician

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in environmental engineering technology or related field
  • Certifications: Environmental certifications, safety certifications, equipment-specific certifications
  • Experience: Internships, field work, or entry-level positions are valuable

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Environmental monitoring, data analysis, equipment operation, regulatory knowledge
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, communication, attention to detail, teamwork
  • Behavioral Traits: Environmental consciousness, analytical thinking, reliability

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Technician → Senior Technician → Lead Technologist → Environmental Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as environmental consulting, regulatory compliance, or research
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: Environmental certifications, safety certifications
  • Books: "Environmental Engineering: Fundamentals, Sustainability, Design" by James R. Mihelcic
  • Competitions: Environmental design competitions
  • Communities: Environmental engineering societies, local environmental groups

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Environmental monitoring experience, regulatory knowledge, field work scenarios
  • Portfolio: Environmental reports, monitoring data, project documentation, safety records

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $40,000
  • Median: $62,000
  • P75: $90,000 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Good growth driven by environmental regulations and sustainability initiatives
  • Automation risk is moderate; field work and hands-on skills remain valuable

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Texas, New York
  • Major industries: Engineering services, government, consulting

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and field work
  • May require travel to monitoring sites and outdoor work
  • Emphasis on safety and environmental protection
Related Occupations: Environmental Engineer, Environmental Scientist, Environmental Consultant
High skill overlap with environmental science and engineering
