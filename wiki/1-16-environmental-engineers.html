Environmental Engineers

Job Title: Environmental Engineers
(环境工程师)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $96,820 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~55,600
10-Year Job Growth: 4% (2022-2032), ~2,000 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Environmental Engineers use engineering principles to solve environmental problems, such as pollution control, waste management, and sustainable development.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design pollution control systems, conduct environmental assessments, develop sustainable solutions
  • Collaborative: Work with government agencies, clients, and multidisciplinary teams
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, manage projects, ensure regulatory compliance

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Air and water quality monitoring equipment, testing devices
  • Software: GIS software, MATLAB, AutoCAD, environmental modeling software
  • Programming: Python, R (for data analysis and modeling)

Common Specializations

  • Air Pollution Control Engineer
  • Water Resources Engineer
  • Waste Management Engineer
  • Sustainable Development Engineer
  • Remediation Engineer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in environmental engineering or related field
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license is highly recommended
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or entry-level positions are valuable

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Environmental science, data analysis, project management, regulatory knowledge
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, communication, teamwork, analytical thinking
  • Behavioral Traits: Environmental consciousness, ethical conduct, attention to detail

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Senior Engineer → Principal Engineer → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as environmental policy, consulting, or research
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, environmental certifications
  • Books: "Introduction to Environmental Engineering" by Mackenzie L. Davis and David A. Cornwell
  • Competitions: Environmental design competitions
  • Communities: American Academy of Environmental Engineers & Scientists (AAEES)

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Environmental regulations, project management scenarios, design challenges
  • Portfolio: Project reports, design documents, environmental assessments, publications

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $60,000
  • Median: $96,820
  • P75: $144,810 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Good growth driven by environmental regulations and public focus on sustainability
  • Automation risk is low due to complex problem-solving and regulatory requirements

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Texas, New York
  • Major industries: Engineering services, government, consulting

Work Environment

  • Mix of office and field work
  • May require travel to project sites and outdoor work
  • Emphasis on safety and environmental protection
Related Occupations: Environmental Scientist, Civil Engineer, Urban Planner
High skill overlap with environmental science and civil engineering
