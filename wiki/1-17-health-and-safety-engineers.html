Health and Safety Engineers

Job Title: Health and Safety Engineers
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $99,040 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~28,700
10-Year Job Growth: 4% (2022-2032), ~1,200 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Health and Safety Engineers develop procedures and design systems to protect people from illness and injury and property from damage.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Conduct risk assessments, design safety systems, investigate accidents
  • Collaborative: Work with management, employees, and regulatory agencies
  • Supportive: Provide safety training, prepare reports, ensure compliance with regulations

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Air quality monitors, noise level meters, safety equipment
  • Software: Risk assessment software, safety management systems, data analysis tools
  • Programming: Not typically required, but data analysis skills are useful

Common Specializations

  • Industrial Safety Engineer
  • Process Safety Engineer
  • Construction Safety Engineer
  • Fire Protection Engineer
  • Ergonomist

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in engineering, preferably in occupational health and safety
  • Certifications: Certified Safety Professional (CSP) is highly recommended
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or entry-level positions are valuable

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Risk assessment, safety regulations, data analysis, engineering principles
  • Soft Skills: Communication, problem-solving, attention to detail, teamwork
  • Behavioral Traits: Safety consciousness, ethical conduct, analytical thinking

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Senior Safety Engineer → Safety Manager → Director of Health and Safety
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as environmental engineering, industrial hygiene, or consulting
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: CSP, Certified Industrial Hygienist (CIH)
  • Books: "Safety Engineering: Principles and Practices" by Frank R. Spellman
  • Communities: American Society of Safety Professionals (ASSP)

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Risk assessment scenarios, regulatory knowledge, accident investigation
  • Portfolio: Safety reports, risk assessments, training materials, project documentation

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $60,000
  • Median: $99,040
  • P75: $145,000 (varies by region and industry)

Job Outlook

  • Steady growth driven by workplace safety regulations and focus on employee well-being
  • Automation risk is low due to the need for human judgment and on-site assessment

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Texas, California, Pennsylvania
  • Major industries: Manufacturing, construction, government, consulting

Work Environment

  • Mix of office and field work
  • May require travel to worksites, including factories, construction sites, and mines
  • Emphasis on preventing accidents and ensuring regulatory compliance
Related Occupations: Industrial Hygienist, Environmental Engineer, Construction Manager.
High skill overlap with industrial hygiene and environmental engineering
