Industrial Engineering Technologists and Technicians

Job Title: Industrial Engineering Technologists and Technicians
(工业工程技术员和技术专家)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $65,000 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~68,000
10-Year Job Growth: 2% (2022-2032), ~1,200 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Industrial Engineering Technologists and Technicians assist industrial engineers in devising efficient systems that integrate workers, machines, materials, information, and energy to make a product or provide a service.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Analyze production processes, conduct time studies, implement quality control procedures
  • Collaborative: Work with industrial engineers, production staff, and management
  • Supportive: Prepare reports, collect data, maintain documentation

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Not specific, but familiar with manufacturing and logistics equipment
  • Software: Data analysis software (Excel, Minitab), process mapping tools (Visio), simulation software
  • Programming: Not typically required, but scripting skills (Python, VBA) are a plus

Common Specializations

  • Manufacturing Technician
  • Quality Control Technician
  • Process Improvement Technician
  • Logistics Technician

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in industrial engineering technology or a related field
  • Certifications: Six Sigma Green Belt, Lean Manufacturing certification
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or entry-level positions in manufacturing or logistics

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Process improvement, data analysis, workflow analysis, quality control
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, communication, teamwork, attention to detail
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, efficiency-minded, collaborative

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Technician → Senior Technician → Lead Technologist → Industrial Engineer (with further education)
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as quality assurance, supply chain management, or production supervision
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or team lead roles

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: Six Sigma, Lean, Certified Engineering Technologist (CET)
  • Books: "The Goal: A Process of Ongoing Improvement" by Eliyahu M. Goldratt
  • Communities: Institute of Industrial and Systems Engineers (IISE)

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Process improvement scenarios, data analysis tasks, quality control methods
  • Portfolio: Process improvement projects, data analysis reports, workflow diagrams, quality control charts

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $45,000
  • Median: $65,000
  • P75: $90,000 (varies by region and industry)

Job Outlook

  • Slow growth, with some opportunities in advanced manufacturing and logistics
  • Automation risk is moderate, as process analysis and problem-solving still require human input

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Michigan, California, Texas
  • Major industries: Manufacturing, engineering services, logistics, healthcare

Work Environment

  • Primarily in manufacturing plants, warehouses, or office settings
  • May require working on the factory floor or in a fast-paced logistics environment
  • Emphasis on efficiency, quality, and process optimization
Related Occupations: Industrial Engineer, Quality Control Inspector, Logistics Analyst
High skill overlap with industrial engineering and quality control
