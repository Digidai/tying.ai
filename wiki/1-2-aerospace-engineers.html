Aerospace Engineers

Job Title: Aerospace Engineers
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $126,880 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~58,800
10-Year Job Growth: 6% (2022-2032), ~3,900 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Aerospace Engineers design, develop, and test aircraft, spacecraft, satellites, and missiles.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design aerospace products, conduct simulations and tests, analyze data, ensure compliance with safety and engineering standards
  • Collaborative: Work with multidisciplinary teams, present findings, coordinate with clients and suppliers
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, maintain documentation, mentor junior engineers

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Wind tunnels, flight simulators, structural test rigs
  • Software: CATIA, MATLAB, Simulink, ANSYS, SolidWorks
  • Programming: Python, C++, Fortran (for simulations and modeling)

Common Specializations

  • Aerodynamics Engineer
  • Propulsion Engineer
  • Avionics Engineer
  • Structures Engineer
  • Systems Engineer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in aerospace engineering or related field (mechanical, electrical, etc.)
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license for higher responsibility roles
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or research projects are highly valued

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Aerodynamics, systems engineering, CAD, simulation, mathematics
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, teamwork, communication, project management
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, creativity, attention to detail

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Senior Engineer → Lead Engineer → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as mechanical engineering, systems engineering, or project management
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or engineering management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, FAA certifications
  • Books: "Fundamentals of Aerodynamics" by John D. Anderson
  • Competitions: AIAA Design Competitions, NASA Aeronautics Challenges
  • Communities: AIAA, SAE International, IEEE Aerospace

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Design challenges, failure analysis, teamwork scenarios
  • Portfolio: Design projects, simulation results, technical papers

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $100,000
  • Median: $126,880
  • P75: $168,370 (varies by region and industry)

Job Outlook

  • Growth driven by commercial aviation, defense, and space exploration
  • Automation risk is low due to high technical and creative requirements

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Los Angeles, Seattle, Dallas
  • Major industries: Aerospace manufacturing, federal government, R&D

Work Environment

  • Primarily office and lab settings, some field testing
  • May require overtime to meet project deadlines
  • Emphasis on safety and regulatory compliance
Related Occupations: Mechanical Engineer, Electrical Engineer, Avionics Engineer.
High skill overlap with other engineering disciplines
