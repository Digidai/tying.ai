Landscape Architects

Job Title: Landscape Architects
(景观设计师)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $73,160 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~24,500
10-Year Job Growth: 0% (2022-2032), ~0 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Landscape Architects plan and design landscapes and outdoor spaces for parks, recreational facilities, private homes, and other open spaces.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Create landscape designs, select appropriate plants and materials, oversee construction
  • Collaborative: Work with clients, architects, and engineers
  • Supportive: Prepare site plans, conduct site analysis, manage projects

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Not specific, but familiar with landscaping and construction equipment
  • Software: AutoCAD, SketchUp, Adobe Creative Suite, GIS software, Lumion
  • Programming: Not typically required

Common Specializations

  • Urban Design
  • Environmental Restoration
  • Park and Recreation Planning
  • Residential Design
  • Sustainable Design

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's or Master's degree in landscape architecture
  • Certifications: Licensure is required in all 50 states
  • Experience: Internships and a strong design portfolio are essential

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Design, horticulture, CAD, project management, environmental science
  • Soft Skills: Creativity, communication, problem-solving, visualization
  • Behavioral Traits: Aesthetic sensibility, environmental awareness, collaborative spirit

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Designer → Landscape Architect → Senior Landscape Architect → Principal/Partner
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as urban planning, environmental consulting, or academia
  • Management vs. Expert: Design-focused expert or firm management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: Professional Landscape Architect (PLA) license
  • Books: "Design with Nature" by Ian L. McHarg
  • Competitions: ASLA Student Awards
  • Communities: American Society of Landscape Architects (ASLA)

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Design philosophy, project experience, software proficiency
  • Portfolio: Design drawings, site plans, 3D renderings, built project photos

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $45,000
  • Median: $73,160
  • P75: $115,000 (varies by region and experience)

Job Outlook

  • Little to no change in employment expected, but opportunities exist in sustainable design and urban development
  • Automation risk is low, as creativity and site-specific solutions are key

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Florida, New York
  • Major industries: Architectural and engineering services, government, construction, real estate

Work Environment

  • Mix of office and field work, including site visits
  • Collaboration with a variety of stakeholders
  • Emphasis on aesthetics, functionality, and environmental sustainability
Related Occupations: Architect, Urban Planner, Civil Engineer, Horticulturist
High skill overlap with architecture and urban planning
