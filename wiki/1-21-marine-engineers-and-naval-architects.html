Marine Engineers and Naval Architects

Job Title: Marine Engineers and Naval Architects
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $99,210 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~9,000
10-Year Job Growth: 1% (2022-2032), ~100 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Marine Engineers and Naval Architects design, build, and maintain ships, submarines, and other marine vessels and structures.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design ship hulls and structures, develop propulsion systems, oversee construction and testing
  • Collaborative: Work with shipyards, clients, and regulatory bodies
  • Supportive: Prepare technical drawings, conduct feasibility studies, manage projects

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Not specific, but deep knowledge of marine propulsion, power, and auxiliary systems
  • Software: CAD/CAE software (ShipConstructor, AutoCAD, Rhino3D), CFD software, structural analysis software
  • Programming: Not typically required, but MATLAB or Python can be useful for analysis

Common Specializations

  • Naval Architect (focus on ship design and hydrodynamics)
  • Marine Engineer (focus on ship systems like propulsion and power)
  • Offshore Structures Engineer
  • Marine Systems Engineer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in marine engineering or naval architecture
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license is highly recommended
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs with shipyards or engineering firms are crucial

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Naval architecture, marine engineering, fluid dynamics, structural analysis, CAD
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, communication, project management, teamwork
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, attention to detail, safety-conscious

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Senior Engineer → Project Manager → Chief Engineer/Naval Architect
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as offshore energy, maritime consulting, or government service
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or project management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License in Naval Architecture and Marine Engineering
  • Books: "Principles of Naval Architecture" by Edward V. Lewis
  • Communities: Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers (SNAME), American Society of Naval Engineers (ASNE)

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Ship design principles, systems engineering questions, project experience
  • Portfolio: Design projects, technical drawings, analysis reports, sea trial data

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $65,000
  • Median: $99,210
  • P75: $149,000 (varies by region and experience)

Job Outlook

  • Slow growth, tied to shipbuilding industry cycles and government contracts
  • Automation risk is low due to the complexity and custom nature of ship design

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Virginia, California, Florida, Washington (areas with major shipyards)
  • Major industries: Shipbuilding, engineering services, government (Navy, Coast Guard), oil and gas

Work Environment

  • Mix of office and on-site work at shipyards or on vessels
  • May require travel and working in industrial environments
  • Emphasis on large-scale project management, safety, and regulatory compliance
Related Occupations: Mechanical Engineer, Aerospace Engineer, Civil Engineer.
