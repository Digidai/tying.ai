Mechanical Engineering Technologists and Technicians

Job Title: Mechanical Engineering Technologists and Technicians
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $62,000 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~45,000
10-Year Job Growth: 2% (2022-2032), ~1,000 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Mechanical Engineering Technologists and Technicians assist mechanical engineers in the design, development, testing, and manufacturing of mechanical devices, including tools, engines, and machines.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Create CAD models, build prototypes, conduct tests, analyze data
  • Collaborative: Work with mechanical engineers, manufacturing staff, and suppliers
  • Supportive: Prepare technical drawings, maintain lab equipment, document test results

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: CNC machines, 3D printers, testing equipment (e.g., tensile testers, vibration tables)
  • Software: CAD software (SolidWorks, AutoCAD, CATIA), CAM software, FEA software (Ansys, Abaqus)
  • Programming: Not typically required, but G-code or scripting can be useful

Common Specializations

  • CAD Technician
  • Automotive Technician
  • HVAC Technician
  • Manufacturing Technician
  • Test Technician

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in mechanical engineering technology or a related field
  • Certifications: Certified SolidWorks Associate (CSWA), Certified Engineering Technologist (CET)
  • Experience: Internships, apprenticeships, or hands-on experience in a machine shop or lab

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Mechanical design, CAD, manufacturing processes, testing, troubleshooting
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, attention to detail, teamwork, communication
  • Behavioral Traits: Hands-on, practical, analytical

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Technician → Senior Technician → Technologist → Mechanical Engineer (with further education)
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields like quality control, manufacturing supervision, or technical sales
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or team lead roles

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: CSWA/CSWP, CET, NIMS (machining) certifications
  • Books: "Machinery's Handbook" by Erik Oberg
  • Competitions: SAE International student competitions, SkillsUSA
  • Communities: American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME)

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: CAD proficiency tests, troubleshooting scenarios, questions about manufacturing processes
  • Portfolio: CAD models, technical drawings, photos of built projects, test reports

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $40,000
  • Median: $62,000
  • P75: $90,000 (varies by industry and region)

Job Outlook

  • Slow growth, with opportunities tied to manufacturing and R&D activities.
  • Automation risk is moderate, as roles shift towards operating and maintaining automated systems.

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Michigan, California, Texas, Ohio
  • Major industries: Manufacturing (especially automotive and machinery), engineering services, R&D

Work Environment

  • Primarily in manufacturing plants, R&D labs, or engineering offices
  • Often involves hands-on work with machinery and equipment
  • Emphasis on precision, quality, and practical problem-solving
Related Occupations: Mechanical Engineer, Machinist, Drafter, Industrial Engineering Technician
High skill overlap with mechanical engineering and drafting
