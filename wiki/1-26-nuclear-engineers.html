Nuclear Engineers

Job Title: Nuclear Engineers
(核工程师)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $122,480 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~15,500
10-Year Job Growth: -3% (2022-2032), ~-500 jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Nuclear Engineers research and develop the processes, instruments, and systems used to derive benefits from nuclear energy and radiation. They are involved in designing, operating, and decommissioning nuclear power plants, as well as in applications for medicine, industry, and national security.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design nuclear reactors, conduct safety analyses, monitor power plant operations
  • Collaborative: Work with physicists, plant operators, and regulatory agencies (e.g., NRC)
  • Supportive: Develop safety procedures, manage nuclear waste, conduct research

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Nuclear reactors, radiation detection instruments, particle accelerators
  • Software: Reactor simulation codes (MCNP, SCALE), thermal-hydraulic analysis software, data analysis tools
  • Programming: Fortran, C++, Python for simulation and data analysis

Common Specializations

  • Reactor Operations and Safety
  • Nuclear Waste Management
  • Radiation Protection (Health Physics)
  • Nuclear Fusion and Plasma Physics
  • Medical Physics

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: A Bachelor's degree in nuclear engineering is standard. A Master's or PhD is often required for research and advanced positions.
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license is highly valuable. Senior Reactor Operator (SRO) license for plant operations.
  • Experience: Internships at national labs, power plants, or government agencies are critical due to the specialized nature of the field. Security clearance is often required.

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Nuclear physics, reactor theory, thermodynamics, radiation protection, safety analysis
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, attention to detail, communication, teamwork, composure under pressure
  • Behavioral Traits: High ethical standards, safety-conscious, analytical mindset

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Engineer → Senior Engineer → Systems Engineer/Operations Manager → Plant Manager/Chief Nuclear Officer
  • Horizontal: Move into regulatory affairs, policy advising, medical physics, or national security
  • Management vs. Expert: Deep technical expert in reactor physics or materials, or management of plant operations or R&D.

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, SRO License, Certified Health Physicist (CHP)
  • Books: "Nuclear Reactor Analysis" by James J. Duderstadt and Louis J. Hamilton
  • Communities: American Nuclear Society (ANS), Nuclear Energy Institute (NEI)

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Reactor physics problems, safety culture questions, regulatory knowledge
  • Portfolio: Research papers, safety analysis reports, simulation results, design projects

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $80,000
  • Median: $122,480
  • P75: $170,000 (varies by sector and experience)

Job Outlook

  • Overall employment is projected to decline as some plants close. However, opportunities exist in decommissioning, waste management, and the development of next-generation reactors (e.g., SMRs, fusion).
  • Many current nuclear engineers are nearing retirement, creating replacement needs.

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: South Carolina, Virginia, Pennsylvania, Tennessee (areas with power plants and national labs)
  • Major industries: Electric power generation, federal government (especially DOE and DOD), R&D, engineering services

Work Environment

  • Highly regulated and safety-focused environments, such as nuclear power plants or national laboratories
  • Requires adherence to strict procedures and protocols
  • Work is often team-based and involves complex, large-scale projects
Related Occupations: Physicist, Mechanical Engineer, Electrical Engineer, Health Physicist
High skill overlap with physics and other core engineering disciplines.
