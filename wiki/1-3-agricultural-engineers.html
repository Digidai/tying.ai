Agricultural Engineers

Job Title: Agricultural Engineers
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $83,460 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~1,300
10-Year Job Growth: 6% (2022-2032), ~600 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Agricultural Engineers design agricultural machinery, equipment, and systems for food production, environmental sustainability, and resource management.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design and test agricultural equipment, develop irrigation and drainage systems, optimize food processing operations
  • Collaborative: Work with farmers, agronomists, and environmental scientists, manage projects, present technical findings
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, ensure regulatory compliance, provide training and support

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Tractors, harvesters, irrigation systems, sensors
  • Software: AutoCAD, MATLAB, GIS software, simulation tools
  • Programming: Python, R (for data analysis and modeling)

Common Specializations

  • Irrigation Engineer
  • Food Processing Engineer
  • Environmental Engineer (Agriculture)

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in agricultural engineering or related field
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license for advanced roles
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or fieldwork are highly valued

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Mechanical design, environmental engineering, project management, data analysis
  • Soft Skills: Communication, teamwork, problem-solving, adaptability
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, creativity, attention to detail

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Project Engineer → Senior Engineer → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into environmental consulting, food production, or research roles
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, Certified Agricultural Engineer
  • Books: "Introduction to Agricultural Engineering Technology" by Field & Solie
  • Competitions: ASABE Student Design Competitions
  • Communities: ASABE, IEEE Agriculture

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Design challenges, sustainability scenarios, teamwork examples
  • Portfolio: Equipment designs, project reports, fieldwork documentation

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $60,000
  • Median: $83,460
  • P75: $120,000 (varies by region and industry)

Job Outlook

  • Growth driven by demand for sustainable food production and environmental solutions
  • Automation risk is moderate; fieldwork and design require human expertise

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot regions: Midwest US, California, Texas
  • Major industries: Agriculture, food production, environmental consulting

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and fieldwork
  • May require travel to farms and production sites
  • Emphasis on safety and environmental regulations
Related Occupations: Environmental Engineer, Mechanical Engineer, Food Scientist.
High skill overlap with environmental and mechanical engineering
