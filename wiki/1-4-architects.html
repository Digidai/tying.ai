Architects

Job Title: Architects
(建筑师)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $82,340 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~112,600
10-Year Job Growth: 5% (2022-2032), ~3,200 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Architects design buildings and structures, ensuring they are functional, safe, and aesthetically pleasing.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design buildings, create detailed plans, coordinate with engineers and contractors
  • Collaborative: Meet with clients, work with construction teams, present design concepts
  • Supportive: Prepare specifications, ensure compliance with building codes, manage project timelines

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: 3D printers, laser cutters, model-making tools
  • Software: AutoCAD, Revit, SketchUp, Adobe Creative Suite
  • Programming: Python, Grasshopper (for parametric design)

Common Specializations

  • Residential Architect
  • Commercial Architect
  • Landscape Architect
  • Interior Architect

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in architecture (B.Arch) or Master's degree (M.Arch)
  • Certifications: State licensure required to practice architecture
  • Experience: Internship and apprenticeship programs are required for licensure

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Design, CAD, project management, building codes
  • Soft Skills: Communication, creativity, problem-solving, teamwork
  • Behavioral Traits: Attention to detail, artistic vision, analytical thinking

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Architect → Project Architect → Senior Architect → Principal
  • Horizontal: Move into interior design, urban planning, or construction management
  • Management vs. Expert: Studio management or design specialist tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: NCARB certification, LEED certification
  • Books: "Architecture: Form, Space, and Order" by Francis D.K. Ching
  • Competitions: AIA Design Awards, Architizer A+ Awards
  • Communities: AIA, NCARB, local architectural societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Design process, project challenges, client communication
  • Portfolio: Design projects, technical drawings, 3D models, project documentation

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $62,000
  • Median: $82,340
  • P75: $137,620 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Steady growth in commercial and residential construction
  • Automation risk is low due to creative and client-facing nature

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: New York, California, Texas
  • Major industries: Architecture firms, construction, real estate development

Work Environment

  • Primarily office-based with site visits
  • May require overtime to meet project deadlines
  • Emphasis on creativity and client satisfaction
Related Occupations: Interior Designer, Urban Planner, Construction Manager
High skill overlap with design and construction fields
