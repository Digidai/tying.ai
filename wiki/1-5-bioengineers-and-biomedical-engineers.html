Bioengineers and Biomedical Engineers

Job Title: Bioengineers and Biomedical Engineers
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $97,410 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~19,300
10-Year Job Growth: 10% (2022-2032), ~1,700 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Bioengineers and Biomedical Engineers apply engineering principles to biological and medical systems, designing medical devices, prosthetics, and diagnostic equipment.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design medical devices, conduct research, develop prosthetics, test biomaterials
  • Collaborative: Work with healthcare professionals, research teams, regulatory agencies
  • Supportive: Prepare technical documentation, ensure regulatory compliance, conduct clinical trials

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: 3D printers, microscopes, testing equipment, bioreactors
  • Software: MATLAB, SolidWorks, COMSOL, LabVIEW
  • Programming: Python, R, C++ (for simulations and data analysis)

Common Specializations

  • Medical Device Engineer
  • Prosthetics Engineer
  • Tissue Engineer
  • Biomechanics Engineer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in biomedical engineering or related field
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license for advanced roles
  • Experience: Internships, research projects, or co-op programs are highly valued

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Biomechanics, medical device design, biomaterials, regulatory compliance
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, teamwork, communication, attention to detail
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, creativity, ethical awareness

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Project Engineer → Senior Engineer → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into research, regulatory affairs, or clinical engineering
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, Certified Biomedical Equipment Technician
  • Books: "Biomedical Engineering: Bridging Medicine and Technology" by W. Mark Saltzman
  • Competitions: BMES Design Competitions, IEEE EMBS Student Paper Competition
  • Communities: BMES, IEEE EMBS, AAMI

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Design challenges, regulatory knowledge, research experience
  • Portfolio: Research projects, device designs, technical papers, regulatory submissions

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $60,000
  • Median: $97,410
  • P75: $154,750 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Strong growth driven by aging population and medical technology advances
  • Automation risk is low due to complex regulatory and research requirements

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Massachusetts, Texas
  • Major industries: Medical device manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, research institutions

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and research settings
  • May require work with biological materials and clinical trials
  • Emphasis on safety, ethics, and regulatory compliance
Related Occupations: Mechanical Engineer, Electrical Engineer, Research Scientist
High skill overlap with mechanical and electrical engineering
