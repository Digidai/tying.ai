Cartographers and Photogrammetrists

Job Title: Cartographers and Photogrammetrists
(制图师和摄影测量师)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $71,500 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~12,800
10-Year Job Growth: 3% (2022-2032), ~300 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Cartographers and Photogrammetrists create maps and charts using geographic data, aerial photographs, and satellite imagery.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Create maps and charts, analyze geographic data, process aerial and satellite imagery
  • Collaborative: Work with surveyors, engineers, and GIS specialists, present findings to clients
  • Supportive: Maintain databases, ensure data accuracy, prepare technical documentation

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: GPS devices, drones, surveying equipment, high-resolution cameras
  • Software: ArcGIS, QGIS, AutoCAD, ENVI, ERDAS IMAGINE
  • Programming: Python, R, SQL (for spatial data analysis)

Common Specializations

  • GIS Specialist
  • Remote Sensing Specialist
  • Topographic Cartographer
  • Thematic Cartographer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in geography, cartography, or related field
  • Certifications: GIS Professional (GISP) certification is valuable
  • Experience: Internships, fieldwork, or research projects are highly valued

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: GIS, mapping software, data analysis, spatial analysis
  • Soft Skills: Attention to detail, problem-solving, communication
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, precision, creativity

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Cartographer → Senior Cartographer → Lead Cartographer → Cartography Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into GIS analysis, remote sensing, or surveying
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: GISP, ASPRS certification
  • Books: "Cartography: Thematic Map Design" by Borden D. Dent
  • Competitions: ESRI User Conference Map Gallery
  • Communities: NACIS, ASPRS, URISA

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Mapping challenges, data accuracy, software proficiency
  • Portfolio: Map samples, GIS projects, technical reports, spatial analyses

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $40,000
  • Median: $71,500
  • P75: $100,000 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Moderate growth with increasing demand for digital mapping and GIS
  • Automation risk is moderate; creative and analytical skills remain valuable

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Washington DC, California, Texas
  • Major industries: Government, engineering services, scientific research

Work Environment

  • Primarily office-based with some fieldwork
  • May require travel for data collection and site visits
  • Emphasis on accuracy and attention to detail
Related Occupations: Surveyor, GIS Analyst, Geographer
High skill overlap with surveying and geography fields
