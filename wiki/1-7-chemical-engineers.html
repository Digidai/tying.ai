Chemical Engineers

Job Title: Chemical Engineers
(化学工程师)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $106,260 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~26,300
10-Year Job Growth: 8% (2022-2032), ~1,600 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Chemical Engineers design and develop chemical manufacturing processes, ensuring efficiency, safety, and environmental compliance.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design chemical processes, optimize production, ensure safety and environmental compliance
  • Collaborative: Work with operators, technicians, and other engineers, manage projects
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, conduct research, maintain process documentation

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Reactors, distillation columns, heat exchangers, pumps
  • Software: Aspen Plus, HYSYS, MATLAB, AutoCAD
  • Programming: Python, FORTRAN (for process simulation)

Common Specializations

  • Process Engineer
  • Plant Engineer
  • Research Engineer
  • Environmental Engineer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in chemical engineering
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license for advanced roles
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or research projects are highly valued

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Process design, chemical reactions, safety protocols, project management
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, teamwork, communication, attention to detail
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, safety consciousness, adaptability

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Process Engineer → Senior Engineer → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into environmental engineering, materials science, or research
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, Six Sigma certification
  • Books: "Chemical Engineering Design" by Gavin Towler
  • Competitions: AIChE Student Design Competitions
  • Communities: AIChE, ACS, local engineering societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Process design challenges, safety scenarios, teamwork examples
  • Portfolio: Process designs, research projects, technical reports, safety analyses

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $70,000
  • Median: $106,260
  • P75: $169,770 (varies by region and industry)

Job Outlook

  • Strong growth in pharmaceuticals, energy, and environmental sectors
  • Automation risk is moderate; process design and optimization require human expertise

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Texas, California, Louisiana
  • Major industries: Chemical manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, energy

Work Environment

  • Mix of office, lab, and plant settings
  • May require shift work in manufacturing environments
  • Emphasis on safety and environmental regulations
Related Occupations: Mechanical Engineer, Environmental Engineer, Materials Engineer
High skill overlap with mechanical and environmental engineering
