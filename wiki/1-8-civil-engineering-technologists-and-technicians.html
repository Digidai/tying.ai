Civil Engineering Technologists and Technicians

Job Title: Civil Engineering Technologists and Technicians
(土木工程技术员和技术专家)
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Associate's degree
Median Annual Wage: $58,000 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~73,000
10-Year Job Growth: 2% (2022-2032), ~1,200 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Civil Engineering Technologists and Technicians assist civil engineers in planning, designing, and overseeing construction and maintenance of infrastructure projects.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Assist in design and planning, conduct field surveys, prepare technical drawings
  • Collaborative: Work with engineers, contractors, and surveyors, participate in project meetings
  • Supportive: Prepare reports, maintain project documentation, ensure compliance with specifications

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Surveying equipment, GPS devices, testing equipment, drones
  • Software: AutoCAD, Civil 3D, MicroStation, GIS software
  • Programming: Basic scripting for automation and data analysis

Common Specializations

  • Structural Technician
  • Transportation Technician
  • Water Resources Technician
  • Geotechnical Technician

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Associate's degree in civil engineering technology or related field
  • Certifications: Optional certifications in surveying or CAD are valuable
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or fieldwork are highly recommended

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: CAD, surveying, technical drawing, project management
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, teamwork, communication, attention to detail
  • Behavioral Traits: Analytical thinking, reliability, adaptability

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Junior Technician → Senior Technician → Lead Technologist → Project Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into surveying, construction management, or related engineering fields
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: Surveying certifications, CAD certifications
  • Books: "Civil Engineering Technology" by John C. Ries
  • Competitions: ASCE Student Design Competitions
  • Communities: ASCE, local engineering societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Technical drawing skills, fieldwork experience, project coordination
  • Portfolio: Technical drawings, project reports, fieldwork documentation, CAD samples

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $40,000
  • Median: $58,000
  • P75: $80,000 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Moderate growth with infrastructure development and maintenance needs
  • Automation risk is moderate; fieldwork and technical skills remain valuable

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Texas, Florida
  • Major industries: Engineering services, construction, government

Work Environment

  • Mix of office and fieldwork
  • May require travel to project sites and outdoor work
  • Emphasis on safety and regulatory compliance
Related Occupations: Civil Engineer, Surveyor, Construction Manager
High skill overlap with civil engineering and surveying
