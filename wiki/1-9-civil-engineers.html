Civil Engineers

Job Title: Civil Engineers
Occupation Group: Architecture & Engineering Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $89,040 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~318,300
10-Year Job Growth: 5% (2022-2032), ~21,200 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Civil Engineers design, build, and maintain infrastructure projects including roads, buildings, bridges, and water systems.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Design infrastructure projects, analyze structural integrity, oversee construction, ensure safety compliance
  • Collaborative: Work with architects, contractors, and government officials, manage project teams
  • Supportive: Prepare technical reports, conduct site inspections, maintain project documentation

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Surveying equipment, testing devices, construction monitoring tools
  • Software: AutoCAD, Civil 3D, SAP2000, ETABS, STAAD.Pro
  • Programming: Python, MATLAB (for analysis and modeling)

Common Specializations

  • Structural Engineer
  • Transportation Engineer
  • Water Resources Engineer
  • Geotechnical Engineer
  • Environmental Engineer

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: Bachelor's degree in civil engineering
  • Certifications: Professional Engineer (PE) license required for higher responsibility roles
  • Experience: Internships, co-op programs, or entry-level positions are highly valued

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Structural analysis, project management, CAD, mathematics
  • Soft Skills: Problem-solving, communication, teamwork, leadership
  • Behavioral Traits: Attention to detail, analytical thinking, safety consciousness

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Entry-level Engineer → Project Engineer → Senior Engineer → Engineering Manager
  • Horizontal: Move into related fields such as construction management, urban planning, or consulting
  • Management vs. Expert: Technical specialist or management tracks

Learning Resources

  • Certifications: PE License, LEED certification, PMP
  • Books: "Civil Engineering Reference Manual" by Michael R. Lindeburg
  • Competitions: ASCE Student Design Competitions
  • Communities: ASCE, local engineering societies

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Design challenges, project management scenarios, technical problem-solving
  • Portfolio: Project designs, technical reports, construction documentation, site photos

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $60,000
  • Median: $89,040
  • P75: $138,690 (varies by region and specialization)

Job Outlook

  • Steady growth driven by infrastructure development and maintenance needs
  • Automation risk is low due to complex design and regulatory requirements

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: California, Texas, New York
  • Major industries: Engineering services, construction, government

Work Environment

  • Mix of office and field work
  • May require travel to project sites and outdoor work
  • Emphasis on safety and regulatory compliance
Related Occupations: Architect, Construction Manager, Surveyor
High skill overlap with architecture and construction management
