Musicians and Singers

音乐家和歌手

Quick Facts

  • Median Salary: Varies greatly; often paid per project (Median hourly wage is approx. $31.00)
  • Typical Education: No formal educational credential required
  • Work Experience: None
  • On-the-job Training: Long-term on-the-job training
  • Job Outlook: 1% (Little or no change)

Job Description

Musicians and singers are performers who use their talents to entertain audiences. Musicians play one or more musical instruments, while singers use their voices. They can work in a huge variety of genres, from classical and jazz to rock and pop. Their work involves countless hours of practice and rehearsal to perfect their craft. They may perform live in concert halls, arenas, clubs, and theaters, or work in recording studios. Many also teach music to supplement their income.

Career Path

There is no standard career path for musicians and singers. Success is based on talent, dedication, and often, business savvy. While many artists are self-taught, formal training through private lessons or a university music program is common. Building a career involves constant practice, networking, and performing, often starting with small, local gigs. As they gain recognition, they may secure recording contracts, join major orchestras, or tour nationally or internationally. Many musicians and singers are self-employed, managing their own careers.

Market Trends

The music industry has been transformed by digital technology. While album sales have declined, streaming services have become the dominant way people listen to music, changing the revenue model for artists. Live performance remains a critical source of income. The internet and social media allow independent artists to build a fanbase and distribute their music directly to listeners without the need for a major record label. This has created more opportunities but also a highly saturated and competitive market. Versatility and the ability to self-promote are essential skills for modern musicians and singers.
