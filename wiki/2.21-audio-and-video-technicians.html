Audio and Video Technicians

Quick Facts

  • Median Salary: Approx. $50,000 per year
  • Typical Education: Associate's degree or postsecondary nondegree award
  • Work Experience: None
  • On-the-job Training: Moderate-term on-the-job training
  • Job Outlook: 13% (Much faster than average)

Job Description

Audio and Video (AV) Technicians are the experts who manage the sound and visuals for a wide range of events. They set up, operate, and maintain the equipment used to record, amplify, mix, and display sound and images. You can find them working at live concerts, sporting events, corporate conferences, in schools, hotels, and courtrooms. Their job is to ensure that the audience can see and hear the event clearly and effectively.

Career Path

Many AV technicians earn an associate's degree or a certificate in audiovisual technology or a related field. Practical, hands-on experience is crucial. Entry-level positions often involve setting up and breaking down equipment. With experience, technicians can specialize in audio, video, or lighting, and may advance to lead technician or AV manager roles, overseeing all technical aspects of a production or for a specific venue. Certifications, like the Certified Technology Specialist (CTS), can also aid in career advancement.

Market Trends

The demand for AV technicians is growing much faster than average, driven by the increasing use of audiovisual technology in business, education, and entertainment. Organizations are continually looking for new ways to engage audiences through high-quality presentations and virtual meetings. This has created a strong need for skilled technicians who can manage complex, integrated AV systems. A deep understanding of IT networking is becoming increasingly important as more AV equipment is connected and controlled over computer networks.
