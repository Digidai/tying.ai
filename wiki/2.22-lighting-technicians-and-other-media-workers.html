Category Overview

This category focuses on the specialists who manage lighting and other essential equipment for film, television, and live productions.

Lighting Technicians

Lighting technicians are responsible for setting up, operating, and maintaining the lighting equipment for a production. They work closely with the director and director of photography to create the desired mood and visibility. This involves rigging lights, programming lighting control boards, and ensuring all equipment is functioning safely and correctly.

Other Media Equipment Workers

This group includes a variety of other essential on-set roles, such as:

  • Grips: Who are responsible for setting up, rigging, and striking all equipment that supports cameras, including tripods, dollies, tracks, and cranes.
  • Teleprompter Operators: Who operate the device that displays scripts for speakers or performers to read.

These roles require technical knowledge, physical stamina, and the ability to work effectively as part of a large production crew.

Career and Market Outlook

Most workers in these roles receive on-the-job training, often starting as production assistants or apprentices. While formal education is not always required, degrees or certifications in film production or theater technology can be beneficial. These are typically freelance, project-based careers, and building a strong network of contacts is essential for finding consistent work. As productions become more technically ambitious, there is a demand for technicians who are skilled with the latest automated lighting systems, remote camera rigs, and other advanced equipment.
