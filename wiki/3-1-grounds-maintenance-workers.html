Grounds Maintenance Workers

Job Title: Grounds Maintenance Workers
Occupation Group: Building & Grounds Cleaning Occupations
Entry-Level Education: No formal educational credential
Median Annual Wage: $37,770 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~1,250,000
10-Year Job Growth: 6% (2022-2032), ~74,900 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Grounds Maintenance Workers are responsible for the upkeep of landscapes and outdoor areas for businesses, parks, and private homes. They perform a wide range of tasks to ensure that grounds are healthy, clean, safe, and attractive.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Mowing lawns, trimming hedges, edging sidewalks, and weeding garden beds.
  • Collaborative: Work in teams to complete large landscaping projects, coordinate with property managers.
  • Supportive: Operate and maintain landscaping equipment, apply pesticides or fertilizers, and clear debris.

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Machines: Lawn mowers (riding and walk-behind), leaf blowers, hedge trimmers, chainsaws, sprinklers.
  • Software: Not typically required, but some may use scheduling or routing software.
  • Other: Hand tools like shovels, rakes, and pruners.

Common Specializations

  • Residential Landscaping
  • Commercial Groundskeeping
  • Golf Course Maintenance
  • Pesticide Handling and Application

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: No formal education is required. On-the-job training is most common.
  • Certifications: While not mandatory, certifications in pesticide application or landscape design can lead to advancement.
  • Experience: Experience with landscaping tasks and equipment operation is highly valued.

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Knowledge of plants, ability to operate machinery, physical stamina and strength.
  • Soft Skills: Reliability, time management, ability to work independently or in a team.
  • Behavioral Traits: Strong work ethic, attention to detail, appreciation for outdoor work.

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Crew Member → Crew Leader → Supervisor → Grounds Manager.
  • Horizontal: Specialize in irrigation, arboriculture, or start their own landscaping business.

Learning Resources

  • Communities: National Association of Landscape Professionals (NALP).
  • Training: Vocational schools and community colleges often offer courses in horticulture or landscape design.

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: "How do you handle working in adverse weather conditions?"
  • Portfolio: A portfolio of past work (photos of maintained landscapes) can be beneficial for those seeking design-focused roles.

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $30,000
  • Median: $37,770
  • P75: $47,000

Job Outlook

  • Employment is projected to grow faster than the average for all occupations. Demand is driven by the need to maintain public parks, corporate campuses, and residential properties.
  • Many job openings will arise from the need to replace workers who transfer to other occupations or exit the labor force.

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot spots: States with year-round warm climates like Florida and California have high employment.
  • Major industries: Services to buildings and dwellings, local government, amusement and recreation industries.

Work Environment

  • Almost exclusively outdoors, in all weather conditions.
  • The work is physically demanding and can be noisy.
  • Work is often seasonal, with longer hours in the spring and summer.
Related Occupations: Landscaper, Gardener, Tree Trimmer, Pest Control Worker.
