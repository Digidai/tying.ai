Pest Control Workers

Job Title: Pest Control Workers
(害虫防治工人)
Occupation Group: Building & Grounds Cleaning Occupations
Entry-Level Education: High school diploma or equivalent
Median Annual Wage: $40,960 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~75,000
10-Year Job Growth: 3% (2022-2032), ~2,300 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Pest control workers inspect buildings and surrounding property for signs of pests, determine the type of treatment needed, and apply pesticides or other techniques to remove them. They play a crucial role in protecting public health and property.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Inspecting properties for infestations, identifying pests (e.g., insects, rodents), and applying or setting traps and chemicals.
  • Collaborative: Educate clients on how to prevent future pest problems, coordinate with property managers.
  • Supportive: Maintain equipment, keep records of services provided, and follow safety regulations for chemical use.

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Equipment: Sprayers, dusters, bait guns, traps.
  • Chemicals: Insecticides, rodenticides, and other pesticides.
  • Software: Scheduling and billing software, route planning applications.
  • Other: Personal protective equipment (PPE) like gloves, respirators, and eye protection.

Common Specializations

  • General Pest Control (residential and commercial)
  • Termite Control
  • Fumigation
  • Wildlife Control

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: A high school diploma is typically required.
  • Certifications: Most states require pest control workers to be licensed. This usually involves passing an exam and may require training in pesticide safety.
  • Experience: On-the-job training under a licensed technician is the standard entry path.

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Knowledge of pest biology and behavior, safe pesticide application, ability to follow complex instructions.
  • Soft Skills: Customer service, problem-solving, attention to detail, physical fitness.
  • Behavioral Traits: Integrity, self-reliance, composure in dealing with pests and clients.

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Technician-in-training → Licensed Technician → Supervisor or Branch Manager.
  • Horizontal: Specialize in a certain type of pest, move into sales, or start their own pest control business.

Learning Resources

  • Communities: National Pest Management Association (NPMA).
  • Training: State-level pest control associations often provide training and certification resources.

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: "How would you handle a severe cockroach infestation in a restaurant?", "What safety precautions are most important in this job?"

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $34,000
  • Median: $40,960
  • P75: $52,000

Job Outlook

  • Employment is projected to grow about as fast as the average.
  • Demand is steady, as pests continue to be a problem in homes, businesses, and agricultural settings.
  • Growing public health concerns and stricter environmental regulations may increase demand for professional pest management.

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Job opportunities are available nationwide.
  • Major industries: Services to buildings and dwellings, local government.

Work Environment

  • Work is performed in a variety of settings, from private homes to large commercial buildings and outdoor areas.
  • May involve working in tight spaces, at heights, or in unpleasant conditions.
  • Requires strict adherence to safety protocols when handling chemicals.
Related Occupations: Grounds Maintenance Worker, Agricultural Worker, Hazardous Materials Removal Worker.
