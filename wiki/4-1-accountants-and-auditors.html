Accountants and Auditors

Job Title: Accountants and Auditors
Occupation Group: Business & Financial Occupations
Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree
Median Annual Wage: $79,880 (US, 2023)
Number Employed: ~1,500,000
10-Year Job Growth: 4% (2022-2032), ~67,400 new jobs

1. What They Do

Definition: Accountants and auditors prepare and examine financial records. They ensure that financial records are accurate and that taxes are paid properly and on time. They assess financial operations and work to help ensure that organizations run efficiently.

Typical Daily Tasks

  • Core: Preparing financial statements, recording and analyzing financial transactions, reconciling bank statements.
  • Collaborative: Advise management on financial decisions, work with auditors and tax authorities.
  • Supportive: Develop and maintain financial databases, ensure compliance with financial regulations.

Tools & Tech Stack

  • Software: Accounting software (QuickBooks, SAP, Oracle), spreadsheet software (Microsoft Excel), data analytics software (Tableau, Power BI).
  • Standards: Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS).
  • Programming: SQL for data extraction, Python for automation and analysis (increasingly common).

Common Specializations

  • Public Accounting (Auditing, Tax, Advisory)
  • Management (or Corporate) Accounting
  • Governmental Accounting
  • Forensic Accounting
  • Internal Auditing

2. Entry & Growth

How to Become One

  • Education: A bachelor's degree in accounting or a related field is required.
  • Certifications: The Certified Public Accountant (CPA) is the most common and valuable certification. Other certifications include Certified Management Accountant (CMA) and Certified Internal Auditor (CIA).
  • Experience: Internships are highly recommended. Many public accounting firms hire directly from university programs.

Key Competencies

  • Hard Skills: Financial analysis, GAAP, tax law, risk assessment, data analysis.
  • Soft Skills: Attention to detail, analytical skills, communication, integrity, problem-solving.
  • Behavioral Traits: Ethical, organized, detail-oriented, able to work with deadlines.

Career Pathways

  • Vertical: Staff Accountant → Senior Accountant → Accounting Manager → Controller → Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
  • Horizontal: Move from public accounting to a corporate role, or specialize in areas like international tax or IT audit.

Learning Resources

  • Communities: American Institute of CPAs (AICPA), Institute of Management Accountants (IMA), The Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA).
  • Books: "Financial Accounting For Dummies", "The Intelligent Investor" (for financial context).

Interview & Portfolio

  • Common interview questions: Technical questions on accounting principles, case studies on financial statements, behavioral questions about ethics and teamwork.
  • Portfolio: Not typical, but certifications and a strong academic record serve a similar purpose.

3. Market & Trends

Wage Distribution

  • P25: $60,000
  • Median: $79,880
  • P75: $105,000

Job Outlook

  • Employment is projected to grow as fast as the average for all occupations.
  • Globalization, a growing economy, and a complex tax and regulatory environment are expected to continue to lead to strong demand for accountants and auditors.
  • Technology is changing the role, with automation handling routine tasks, allowing accountants to focus more on advisory and analytical work.

Geographic & Industry Distribution

  • Hot cities: Major financial hubs like New York City, Chicago, and Los Angeles.
  • Major industries: Accounting, tax preparation, bookkeeping, and payroll services; finance and insurance; government.

Work Environment

  • Primarily office-based.
  • Work can be high-pressure with long hours, especially during busy periods like tax season or quarterly closings.
Related Occupations: Financial Analyst, Budget Analyst, Tax Examiner, Personal Financial Advisor.
Shares a strong foundation in finance, mathematics, and analytical skills.
