What is a Health Education Specialist?

A Health Education Specialist is a professionally prepared individual who serves in a variety of roles and is specifically trained to use appropriate educational strategies and methods to facilitate the development of policies, procedures, interventions, and systems conducive to the health of individuals, groups, and communities. They work to promote wellness and prevent disease.

What does a Health Education Specialist do?

Health Education Specialists are involved in the full lifecycle of health promotion programs. Their key responsibilities include:

  • Assessing Needs: Collecting and analyzing data to identify the health needs of a particular population.
  • Planning Programs: Developing health education programs and materials designed to encourage healthy behaviors and environments.
  • Implementation: Carrying out the planned health education programs in various settings like schools, hospitals, non-profits, and workplaces.
  • Evaluation: Determining the impact and effectiveness of their programs and making adjustments for future initiatives.
  • Advocacy: Advocating for improved health resources and policies that promote public health.
  • Communication: Serving as a reliable source of health information for the community and other health professionals.

What are the skills of a Health Education Specialist?

To successfully develop and manage health programs, these specialists need a strong foundation in public health sciences and excellent communication skills:

  • Public Health Knowledge: A deep understanding of health behavior theories, epidemiology, and program planning models.
  • Communication Skills: The ability to communicate complex health information clearly and persuasively to diverse audiences.
  • Analytical Skills: The skill to assess community needs and evaluate program outcomes with data.
  • Instructional Skills: The ability to create engaging educational materials and present information effectively.
  • Interpersonal Skills: The capacity to work collaboratively with community members, colleagues, and stakeholders from different sectors.

How to become a Health Education Specialist?

A bachelor's degree in health education, public health, or a related field is the minimum requirement. Many positions, especially at the management level, require a master's degree (MPH or M.S. in Public Health). The Certified Health Education Specialist (CHES) and Master Certified Health Education Specialist (MCHES) are voluntary, but highly regarded, professional credentials that demonstrate competency in the field and are often preferred or required by employers.
