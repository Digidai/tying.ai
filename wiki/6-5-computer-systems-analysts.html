What is a Computer Systems Analyst?

A Computer Systems Analyst acts as a bridge between an organization's business functions and its Information Technology (IT) department. They study an organization's current computer systems and business processes to find ways to improve them. Their goal is to ensure that the IT systems are helping the organization operate as efficiently and effectively as possible.

What do they do?

Systems analysts are strategic thinkers who align technology with business goals. Their key responsibilities include:

  • Analysis: Examining existing computer systems and business models to identify areas for improvement.
  • Consultation: Meeting with business managers to discuss their needs and with the IT department to understand system capabilities.
  • Solution Design: Designing new IT solutions, which could involve new hardware, software, or a combination of both, to meet business requirements.
  • Cost-Benefit Analysis: Researching the costs and benefits of new projects and presenting a business case to management.
  • Overseeing Implementation: Working with programmers and engineers to oversee the installation and testing of new systems.
  • Training: Helping to train users on the new systems and procedures.

What are their skills?

This role requires a unique combination of business acumen and technical expertise. Key skills include:

  • Analytical Skills: The ability to understand complex business needs and how technology can be used to address them.
  • Business Knowledge: A strong understanding of business principles and practices in the industry they work in.
  • Technical Skills: A good knowledge of computer hardware, software, and networking.
  • Communication Skills: The ability to communicate effectively with both technical staff and non-technical business managers.
  • Problem-Solving Skills: The creativity to design innovative and effective technology solutions for business problems.

How to become one?

A bachelor's degree in a computer-related field, such as computer science or information systems, is typical. However, because this role bridges business and IT, some analysts have degrees in business or management information systems (MIS). Many employers prefer candidates who have a combination of technical knowledge and business experience. Continuous learning is essential to keep up with the fast-paced changes in technology.
