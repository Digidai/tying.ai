What is a Network and Computer Systems Administrator?

Network and Computer Systems Administrators, often called sysadmins, are the IT professionals who ensure that an organization's computer networks and systems are running efficiently and reliably. They are responsible for the daily upkeep, configuration, and operation of computer networks, including hardware, software, and data. They are essential to nearly every organization that uses computers to conduct business.

What do they do?

Administrators handle the hands-on work of keeping IT infrastructure online. Their responsibilities are broad and include:

  • System Installation and Configuration: Setting up and configuring servers, computers, and network hardware like routers and switches.
  • Maintenance and Upkeep: Performing regular maintenance, applying software patches and updates, and ensuring systems are running optimally.
  • Troubleshooting: Diagnosing and resolving network and system issues as they arise, often as an escalation point for the help desk.
  • User Management: Adding, removing, and updating user account information, and managing access permissions.
  • Performance Monitoring: Monitoring network and system performance to identify potential problems before they cause outages.
  • Security: Implementing and maintaining security measures, such as firewalls and antivirus software, in coordination with security analysts.

What are their skills?

To keep complex IT environments running smoothly, administrators need strong technical and organizational skills:

  • Technical Proficiency: A strong understanding of network infrastructure, server operating systems (like Windows Server or Linux), and virtualization technologies.
  • Problem-Solving Skills: The ability to quickly diagnose and resolve technical problems under pressure.
  • Analytical Skills: The skill to monitor system performance and anticipate future needs or potential issues.
  • Communication Skills: The ability to communicate with users and other IT staff to resolve issues and explain procedures.
  • Organizational Skills: The ability to manage multiple tasks and projects simultaneously.

How to become one?

The educational requirements for these roles can vary. Some positions may require a bachelor's degree in a computer-related field, while others may be open to candidates with an associate's degree or postsecondary certificate. Practical experience is highly valued. Many administrators start in entry-level IT roles like help desk support. Professional certifications from vendors like Microsoft (Microsoft Certified: Azure Administrator Associate), Cisco (CCNA), and CompTIA (CompTIA Server+, CompTIA Network+) are very common and can be critical for career advancement.
